The Different Types of Ski Slopes

The colour coding system in skiing differentiates the beginner, intermediate and expert slopes!

Please Note.. This is the colour system for the european ski slopes. However, countries like North America and Japan will have a completely different system.
  • Green: At an angle of between 0-20% this is perfect for beginners!
  • Blue: This slope between 20-30% is great for beginners who have had a bit of practice, and want to gain a little more speed
  • Red: A red slope should only be tried once you have had much practice and ideally with an experienced skiier. The angle at 30-45% can be a bit of a shock the first time you try! However, it can be great fun if you get used to it!
  • Black: A black slope is incredibly steep and can result in serious injury if tried with little practice. Therefore, it is essential to train yourself to handle the 45% angle, ideally with the use of the red slopes!
